Brand Mark

Do you need the “M”?

Six ways to handle the logo in your header. My recommendation is the simplest one: let your name carry the brand. A small mark is still useful for a favicon or social avatar, so a couple of refined options are here too.

Mona Weathers

A · Wordmark only

No icon. Cleanest and most editorial. Reads as a personal brand, not a company.

My recommendation
MMona Weathers

B · Filled circle “M” (current)

Friendly and clear, but a single-letter badge can feel a touch generic / corporate.

mMona Weathers

C · Outline circle, lowercase

Lighter and more refined than the filled badge. Softer, more boutique feel.

MWMona Weathers

D · “MW” monogram, no container

Initials as a quiet signature beside the name. Works well as a standalone avatar too.

Mona Weathers.

E · Wordmark + signature dot

Name only, with one tiny accent-colored period as a brand signature. Minimal but distinctive.

MWMona Weathers

F · Soft-square “MW” lockup

Rounded tile with both initials. The most “logo-like” option; good favicon/avatar.
